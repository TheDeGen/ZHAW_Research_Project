The foundation of this research project rests upon two complementary data streams: textual news data capturing market sentiment and discourse, and quantitative electri-city market data reflecting actual trading dynamics. The sourcing strategy prioritised comprehensive coverage across both domains whilst maintaining data quality and temporal alignment necessary for signal generation. The multi-modal approach recognises that electricity markets operate at the intersection of physical commodity trading and information-driven financial markets, where news events can trigger immediate price responses whilst fundamental supply-demand dynamics establish longer-term trends (Ziel & Steinert, 2024).

# News Data Collection
The collection of news data employed NewsAPI, an aggregation service that provides programmatic access to historical and current news articles from diverse sources. The service's comprehensive metadata structure, including timestamps, source attributi-on, and article descriptions, makes it particularly suitable for time-series analysis in energy markets where temporal precision is crucial WOOO. Recent applications in commodity market forecasting have demonstrated that NewsAPI's structured data format facilitates the extraction of market-relevant signals from high-frequency news flows.
The news collection strategy encompassed both English- and German-language sources to capture the full spectrum of discourse surrounding German electricity markets. This bilingual approach recognises that whilst German-language sources provide domestic market insights and regulatory announcements, English-language international sources often contain broader market analysis and global energy trends that influence local prices (Digga wo Makro, 2099). The linguistic diversity is signifi-cant given Germany's central role in European electricity markets, where cross-border flows and interconnector capacity create price dependencies across multiple national markets (Digga Deutschland grosser Stromhändler, 2019). Furthermore, international financial news services often provide analytical depth on German eco-nomic and energy policy that complements domestic reporting, offering institutional investor perspectives that can influence market dynamics (Bloomi oder so, 2025).

# Energy Data Collection
The electricity market data was sourced from Energy-Charts.info, a platform main-tained by the Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (ISE) that has become widely used in German electricity market research (Wirth, 2023). This platform ag-gregates data from multiple authoritative sources, including ENTSO-E (European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity), EPEX SPOT, and the four German transmission system operators (50Hertz, Amprion, TenneT, and Trans-netBW), providing a unified interface for accessing comprehensive market data (Fraunhofer ISE, 2024). The platform's integration of multiple data streams enables researchers to access harmonised datasets that would otherwise require complex reconciliation across different source systems (Schmidt et al., 2023).
